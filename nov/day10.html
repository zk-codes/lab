Edge to Edge

designing without inner space

Padding creates breathing room. It separates content from container. We use it constantly, unconsciously. But today, it's forbidden.

This page uses margin and border only. No padding property exists in the stylesheet. The result forces us to think differently about space.

Notice how this box achieves "padding" through a nested element with margin instead. The constraint doesn't eliminate space - it changes how we create it.

Without padding, content touches edges. Buttons feel tighter. Containers become more defined by their borders than their interiors. The relationship between inside and outside shifts.

The box model has four parts: content, padding, border, margin. Remove one, and you must rely more heavily on the others.

Grid and flexbox gaps become essential. Margins do the work that padding once did. Borders gain new prominence. The constraint reveals our dependence on a single property - and shows us alternatives.
