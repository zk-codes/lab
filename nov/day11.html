Beyond the Blank

leaving white behind

The default background of the web is white. Open any browser, load about:blank, and there it is: the empty page, waiting. We've been trained to see white as neutral, as absence, as the starting point.

But white is not nothing. It's a choice. And today, we choose differently.

Eight grays, none of them white. The lightest (#f1f2f6) has a hint of blue. The darkest (#1e272e) has warmth. Each has character that pure white lacks.

"White space is like a window. It allows you to look through things. But sometimes, you want walls."

Screens emit light. On a dark room, a white background becomes a flashlight aimed at your eyes. The off-white, the cream, the slate - these are kindnesses. They reduce strain. They create atmosphere.

The constraint reveals: we reach for white out of habit, not necessity. There's a whole spectrum of "light enough to read" that isn't the blinding default.
