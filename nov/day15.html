The Descent

reading as falling

Scroll down. Keep scrolling. This is the only direction allowed.

No columns sit side by side. No grids spread horizontally. Everything stacks. Everything descends. The page is a single column, a waterfall of content.

Why We Go Sideways

We put things next to each other to compare them. To show relationships. To fill space efficiently. But vertical reading is older, deeper. Scrolls unrolled downward long before books opened sideways.

"The vertical is the axis of contemplation. The horizontal is the axis of comparison."

Mobile design already knows this. On a phone, everything is vertical. The thumb scrolls up and down. The constraint of the small screen became a design language.

Down we go word by word

This layout has no sidebars. No multi-column layouts. No images floating beside text. Just the steady descent of reading, one element after another.

↓

The constraint creates focus. Without horizontal space to fill, content becomes sequence. Time becomes the organizing principle. First this, then that, then the next thing.

Keep scrolling. You're doing it right.
