Short

six or less

Every word on this page has six marks or fewer. No word goes beyond that limit. This is hard. This takes focus. But it works.

Why? Fewer is often more. Short words hit hard. They don't hide. They say what they mean and stop.

The Rule

Each word must fit in six spaces. "Simple" fits. "Simply" does not. You learn to choose your words well. You trim the fat. You find that less is clear.

"The most useful virtue is to be short." — Old saying, made new.

What I Found

Some words I wanted were too long. I had to find new paths. "Write" works, but "writing" does not. "Begin" works, but "beginning" fails. Each block forces a new way.

The limit shapes how you think. You stop and ask: is there a better word? A simpler path? Often, there is.

Note: Every single word here is six marks or fewer. Count them if you wish.

This is prose under rules. But rules create focus. They push you to be direct, to cut, to mean what you say. Try it. You may find that short is not just a limit. It is a gift.
