Flat Color

no gradients, no blending

Every color on this page is solid. No gradual transitions. No smooth blends. Each hue stands alone, confident in its singularity.

Gradients add sophistication. They suggest depth, movement, light sources. But solid color has its own power: clarity, boldness, honesty.

Solid Design

Flat and Bold

Solid colors make statements. No ambiguity, no transition states.

Border Defined

Without gradients, borders become essential for visual separation.

Contrast Heavy

Solid colors demand careful attention to contrast ratios.

"A color is as strong as the impression it creates." — Ivan Albright

Depth can come from layering. This box uses two solid colors at different angles - no gradient needed to create visual interest.

The web's early days were solid. Flat colors in tables, gif backgrounds that tiled. We added gradients as displays improved. But flat design brought solids back into fashion.

This constraint isn't primitive - it's confident. Each color is a decision, not a transition.
