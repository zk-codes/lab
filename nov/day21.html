Without Lines

separation through other means

No borders exist on this page. Not a single border property appears in the stylesheet. Yet elements are still contained, separated, defined.

The Alternatives

Box Shadow

Shadows create depth and separation without explicit lines.

Background

Color differences naturally separate regions of content.

Spacing

White space is the most elegant separator of all.

This section stands apart through background color alone. No lines needed - the eye understands the boundary instantly.

Borders are convenient shorthand. A line says "here is an edge." But the same message can be conveyed through contrast, through space, through shadow.

The gradient pretends to be a border. A thin strip of color at the edge of an element. The eye sees a line; the CSS contains none.

Even the classic outline effect - a box around text - can be achieved with an inset box-shadow. No border required.

The constraint reveals redundancy. We often use borders where background changes would suffice. Where spacing would be cleaner. Where shadows would add depth instead of just lines.
