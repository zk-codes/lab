Transparent

nothing behind the text

No element on this page has a background color. Everything is transparent. The text floats against whatever lies beneath.

Just words.

Without backgrounds, we lose our easiest tool for grouping and separation. But we gain something too: purity. The content becomes everything. The container disappears.

What Remains

Borders still work. They define edges without filling space. This box contains text but contains no color.

Text color creates hierarchy. Faded text recedes. Bold text advances. The same space, different weights.

Shapes can exist as outlines. These forms are defined by their edges, not their fill. The inside is nothing, which is also something.

"Less is more." But this takes it further. Less than less. The background is not white - it is absent.

Hollow letters

The constraint is severe. No cards, no callouts, no highlighted sections - at least not in the traditional sense. But the page still communicates. The eye still finds structure.

Perhaps we paint backgrounds out of habit. Perhaps transparency is not emptiness, but honesty.
