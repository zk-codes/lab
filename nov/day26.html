Three

beginning, middle, end

I. The Constraint

This page contains exactly three sections. Not four. Not two. Three.

The number three has power. Thesis, antithesis, synthesis. Morning, afternoon, night. Birth, life, death. Beginning, middle, end.

Every narrative fits this structure. Every argument can be reduced to it. Three is completeness.

II. The Challenge

Most pages sprawl. Sections multiply like cells dividing. Content expands to fill available space.

But what if you had to stop at three? What would you cut? What would you combine?

The constraint forces ruthless prioritization. Three sections means three main ideas. Everything else is either contained within them or eliminated.

III. The Lesson

Brevity is not limitation. It is focus.

The best presentations have three points. The best stories have three acts. The best pages might have three sections.

You've reached the end. Three was enough.
