Five Notes

a limited palette symphony

A pentatonic scale contains only five notes, yet entire musical traditions have been built upon it. This page contains only five colors. Let's see what we can build.

The palette is drawn from deep navy to soft off-white. A range that evokes moonlit water, steel bridges, winter fog. Each color has a role: background, container, accent, text, highlight.

"The enemy of art is the absence of limitations."

- Orson Welles

Five colors force hierarchy. You cannot rely on infinite variation. Each hue must earn its place. Contrast becomes precious. Repetition becomes rhythm.

Deep Navy Dark Blue Steel Muted Blue Off-White

Consider: most great design uses restraint. Brand guidelines limit palettes. Swiss typography works in two colors. Japanese aesthetics find beauty in reduction.

The constraint is not a cage. It is a framework within which creativity can focus, instead of scatter.
