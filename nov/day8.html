Soft Edges

a world without corners

Nature rarely creates right angles. Rivers curve. Hills roll. Cells are round. The sharp corner is a human invention, an artifact of measurement and manufacture.

On this page, every element curves. No sharp corners exist. The border-radius property transforms everything.

The constraint softens not just the visuals, but the feeling. Sharp edges convey precision, authority, rigidity. Curves suggest welcome, comfort, approachability.

Consider how this changes your experience:

  • Buttons want to be pressed, not clicked
  • Containers feel like they're holding, not trapping
  • The page breathes instead of boxing
  • Reading becomes resting
We shape our tools and thereafter our tools shape us. But what if we shaped them softer?

The rectangle is efficient. It tiles perfectly. It maximizes space. But the curve is human. It follows the path of least resistance. It mimics what we find in the world that existed before screens.
