There are over 150,000 characters in Unicode. Each one a tiny ambassador from some corner of human expression: a letter, a symbol, a pictograph older than most nations.

☽ ⚗ ✧ ⌘ ◐ ⟁ ⧫ ☉ ⊛ ⋈ ⟐ ⚶

Consider U+2603: ☃. A snowman. Standardized across every device on Earth. Someone decided this was important enough to encode forever. They were right.

☆ born from clay tablets ☆
⟳ through papyrus and vellum ⟳
◈ into magnetic storms ◈
∞ we persist in symbols ∞

The Unicode Consortium meets regularly to decide which symbols deserve immortality. Somewhere, in a conference room, humans debate whether a specific emoji variation warrants a permanent code point. This is beautiful. This is absurd. This is civilization.

The library? It isn't just a building full of books. It's the Unicode standard, in physical form. Every single shelf? A code page. Every aisle? A character block.

Like the internet, its complementary successor, it goes on for a glorious forever.

Whether on paper or in binary, the symbols await my brain to render them. Pulling out a book or webpage translates strange pictograms into vivid truth.
