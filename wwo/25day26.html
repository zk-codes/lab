The Feel Of Things

Screens are smooth. Frictionless. Our fingers glide across glass as if touching nothing at all. But the web wasn't always this way.

There was a time when backgrounds had weight. When buttons looked like they could be pressed. When shadows fell convincingly. We built textures into our digital spaces because we missed the roughness of paper, the grain of wood, the weave of cloth.

Now we've stripped it away. Flat design. Clean surfaces. Minimalism as ideology. But texture persists in the margins. In the choice of a serif over sans. In the subtle noise layered beneath solid color. In the way a shadow suggests depth.

Run your eyes across this page. Feel the words settle into something almost tangible. The web can never truly touch us back. But it can remember what touch felt like.
