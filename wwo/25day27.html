Every line of code is an incantation. We write symbols in precise sequences, speak them into machines, and watch reality reshape itself. The compiler is our familiar; the terminal, our scrying glass.

function summon() {
  return magic;

The old spellcasters knew: names have power. To name a thing is to command it. Variables, functions, classes. We baptize our abstractions and they obey.

  • One syntax, precisely followed
  • Intent, clearly stated
  • Patience for debugging
  • The willingness to try again

Every programmer is a witch, a wizard, an alchemist. We transmute logic into action, thought into form. The spells are just text files. But what they summon is real.
