The veil is thin tonight. Between the living web and the dead links. Between the pages that load and the ones that return 404.

Somewhere, a server spins in an abandoned data center. Its pages unseen for years. Ghost traffic from bots that don't know the site has died. This is the true horror of the internet: nothing ever fully disappears, yet nothing truly persists.

We build monuments in HTML. We carve our names in CSS. And when the hosting bill goes unpaid, we join the graveyard of Geocities, of defunct blogs, of abandoned dreams.

But tonight, the dead sites walk again. Hover over the darkness. You might see something looking back.

Happy Halloween. May your builds succeed and your deployments be uneventful. May your links never rot and your backups always restore.

And if you hear typing from an empty room... don't look.
